Definition 2.3.2.

For any matrix \(M\text{,}\) let \((M)_{ij}=m_{ij}\) denote the entry in the \(i\)th row and \(j\)th column. Then, given a \(m\times n\) matrix \(A\) and a \(n\times k\) matrix \(B\text{,}\) we define \(AB\) the product of \(A\) and \(B\) to be a \(m\times k\) matrix where:

\begin{equation*} (AB)_{ij}=\begin{pmatrix}\textcolor{red}{a_{i1}} \amp \textcolor{red}{a_{i2}} \amp \cdots \amp \textcolor{red}{a_{in}} \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix}\textcolor{blue}{b_{1j}} \\ \textcolor{blue}{b_{2j}} \\ \vdots \\ \textcolor{blue}{b_{nj}} \end{pmatrix} =\begin{pmatrix}\textcolor{red}{a_{i1}}\textcolor{blue}{b_{1j}}+\textcolor{red}{a_{i2}}\textcolor{blue}{b_{2j}}+\cdots+\textcolor{red}{a_{in}}\textcolor{blue}{b_{nj}} \end{pmatrix} \end{equation*}
